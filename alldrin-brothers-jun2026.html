Cybersecurity

Protecting Your Accounts Payable Process from AI Fraud

AI-powered impersonation attacks are increasingly targeting Accounts Payable teams — using voice cloning, spoofed emails, and convincing fake requests to trick staff into approving fraudulent payments. The key defense is out-of-band verification: always confirm requests to change bank details or approve urgent payments through a second, independent channel. Never rely on appearance alone. Standardize verification habits across your team, enforce multi-factor authentication on financial systems, and create a culture where pausing on high-risk actions is encouraged — not questioned.

IT Management

SaaS Offboarding: Are "Zombie" Accounts Putting You at Risk?

When employees leave, their SaaS accounts often don't. These dormant "zombie" accounts — in tools like Salesforce, Asana, Notion, and dozens of smaller apps — remain active long after offboarding, creating unnecessary security exposure. A thorough audit starts with pulling a full list of all apps connected to your identity providers, cross-referencing billing records, and reviewing HR exit records against your SaaS inventory. For every remaining active account, ensure MFA is enforced and shared logins are replaced with individual credentials. A quarterly review cadence keeps the problem from building back up.

Tip of the Month — Passkey Migration

Start Your Move to Passwordless Authentication

Transitioning to passkeys doesn't have to happen overnight. Begin by auditing which tools already support passkeys natively, then prioritize rollout for administrators and high-risk users first. Run a parallel phase where passkeys work on enrolled devices while passwords serve as a temporary fallback. For tools that don't yet support passkeys, a password manager bridges the gap. Make sure users understand how passkeys sync across their devices — whether through iCloud Keychain, Google Password Manager, or another platform — so access is never lost during the transition.
