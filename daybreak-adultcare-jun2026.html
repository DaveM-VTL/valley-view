Cybersecurity

Protecting Your Accounts Payable Process from AI Fraud

AI-powered fraud is increasingly targeting accounts payable teams — using voice cloning and spoofed emails to impersonate vendors or executives and redirect payments to fraudulent accounts. These attacks are convincing because they reference real invoice numbers and known contacts, often sourced from prior data breaches or public records. Finance staff should require a separate, verified callback before processing any payment change request, regardless of how legitimate the message appears. Multi-factor authentication on accounting platforms and a two-person approval requirement for payment modifications are among the strongest defenses available. Training staff to recognize urgency-based pressure tactics is equally important.

IT Management

SaaS Offboarding: Are "Zombie" Accounts Putting You at Risk?

When employees leave, their accounts are often disabled — but SaaS subscriptions tied to those accounts frequently remain active and accessible. These "zombie accounts" in tools like Dropbox, DocuSign, or project management platforms continue to incur charges and, more critically, may still hold sensitive data or active integrations. A structured offboarding checklist that includes every SaaS platform the employee accessed is the only reliable way to close these gaps. IT and HR should review the full software roster quarterly to identify unused licenses and dormant access. Failing to do so creates both unnecessary cost and a potential security gap that cyber insurers are increasingly flagging during audits.

Tip of the Month — Passkey Migration

Start Your Move to Passwordless Authentication

Passkeys replace traditional passwords with a cryptographic key pair stored on your device, making phishing virtually impossible and sign-ins faster. Most major platforms — Google, Apple, Microsoft, and many financial institutions — now support passkeys. To get started, go to your account's security settings and look for "Passkeys" or "Sign in with your device." Enabling passkeys on your highest-value accounts — email, banking, and Microsoft 365 — dramatically reduces your exposure to credential theft with almost no added friction for daily use.
